My Dear Didi,

I know that words I said yesterday were not kind. They were sharp, rushed, and unfair — and you deserved none of it. I'm truly sorry.

You are someone who has always been there for me — through my silences, my tantrums, my mistakes, and my victories. You never held a scoreboard. But I let you down with those words, and I own that completely.

I've been thinking about it ever since. Not because I had to — but because hurting you genuinely hurt me too. That's what siblings do, I suppose. We carry each other's pain without realising it.

On this birthday, I want you to know that you are not just celebrated because the calendar says so. You are celebrated because you are strong, fierce, kind, and irreplaceable — and I would be lost without you.

I promise to choose my words more carefully. I promise to be the brother you deserve — not just today, but every day.

Please forgive me. 💕

With all my love,
Your Brother 🌸

✦ A poem, written just for her ✦

Have you seen a mother and daughter of your age,
It is fun and rage, but sometimes a cage…
Here the cage is not what it is,
It is the boundaries and integrity a human should have…
I also have the same,
With all my love I gave her Laado as a nickname…
She is new to me, but she is enough,
Trying to be the best for her, so that her life could be brave through the rough…
I don't know what happened to me in the last few weeks,
Don't want to disclose, but she made me weak…
Weak in the sense, I can't live without her,
Want to take all her discomfort, so that she won't suffer…
Suffering isn't optional in nature,
But my role is to never let her be alone…
Regret will follow the people who left her,
But with all my surety now she is not alone…
It is a proud moment when she calls me bhai,
Trying to keep her happy by being her parchhai,
Met a lot of people, but no such pure soul like her,
But don't know why the Almighty let her only suffer…
This was a part of my life which was random,
Someone became my sister all of a sudden…
If I could reincarnate as a human,
I would like to become your own brother and their next one…
In this one there are many walls,
If there is almighty let him hear my plea…
Even if not as a human soul,
Any organism which could be close to me…
I don't know if she counts on me or not,
I have only a few people and you are one of them.
Just like Ravan fought with God for his sister,
I would do the same even if you don't believe…
Don't know what she feels about me,
But she has been always close to my heart.
Never want to let her go —
Let me disclose her name: Ruchi Agrawal.

— With love, Your Bhai 🌸

A Letter to Laado

Ruchi Agrawal · My friend, my sister, my constant

🌸

Ruchi Agrawal

Friend · Behen · Soul

I have a friend in my life whom I truly consider my sister. I don't label her as younger or older — because sometimes she guides me like an elder sister, and at other times she behaves like a younger one.

In the beginning, she was just my teammate. But one small moment changed everything between us. One day, I was feeling upset and wasn't talking to anyone the way I normally do. Out of everyone around me, she was the only one who noticed. She came up to me and asked, "What happened, Rohan? You seem upset." No one else asked — but she did. That simple moment meant a lot to me.

During college, I had a few female friends, but none of them understood me the way Ruchi Agrawal did. (And yes, even the spelling of her name is a little unique 😅)

Honestly, Ruchi is the kind of friend anyone would feel lucky to have in their life. I feel truly grateful that she is part of mine. I have always seen her as my sister, and she is someone I will always stand by, no matter what.

Ruchi, I love you as a sister. You have made my college life more meaningful and memorable. You have always been there for me, patiently listening to my problems, complaints, and random thoughts.

I don't really know how much I matter in your life, but I want you to know that you are part of my inner circle. I genuinely respect you, admire you, and care deeply about you.

Maybe I don't know my exact place in your life, but I do know that I always want to be there in yours. As a brother, I will always stand by you — especially during tough times, whether the problems are big or small.

There are many things I have shared with you that I haven't shared with anyone else. I hope you understand what that means. Usually, a man shares his deepest thoughts only with the people he trusts the most, and for me, that person is you, Ruchi.

I don't have a sister in my family. Even though I don't strongly believe in God, sometimes it still feels like life gifted me a sister in the form of you.

I only have one request — please always remain my sister and never take our bond for granted. I truly respect you and care about you deeply. I don't expect anything from you except loyalty, and I believe you will never fail in that.

My friend and my Laado, Ruchi Agrawal — I sincerely request you to never take me for granted.

Always yours,
Rohan 🌸
